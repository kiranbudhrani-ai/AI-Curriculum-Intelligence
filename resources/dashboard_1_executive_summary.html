What leadership should take from this

AI has moved from concentrated to catalog-wide. Computing & Informatics still leads with the highest penetration (13.3% of its scanned courses), but AI signals now span 26 disciplines/course types across 6 colleges.

2026 is the inflection point. Of the 89 AI-signal records with a usable effective date, 46 — more than half — became effective in 2026 alone, versus 43 across every prior year combined back to 2017.

Colleges are at different adoption stages. The pattern shows accelerating, expanding, and emerging AI curriculum activity across colleges, suggesting that development is not happening uniformly but is advancing at different speeds and in different forms.

Title visibility understates the footprint. 50 of 117 records (Tier 2) mention AI in the course description, not the title — meaning a title-only scan of the catalog would miss nearly half of where AI activity already lives.
