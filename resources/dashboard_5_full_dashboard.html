Additional growth insights for leadership

What this report measures:
Catalog-level AI signals in course titles and descriptions, plus effective-year timing where usable dates are available.
What this report does not measure:
Enrollment, seat capacity, section offerings, student demand, faculty readiness, course quality, or the depth of AI use in instruction.
How to use this with Deans:
Use the growth views to identify where AI curriculum activity is already visible, where it is emerging, and where additional context from colleges may be needed.

Leadership interpretation: what the signals show, and what they do not show

What this does show:
AI has a visible and embedded footprint across multiple colleges, levels, and course types in the current 2026 course-signal files.
What this does not show:
This is not a measure of learning quality, enrollment, section offerings, seat capacity, student demand, faculty readiness, or depth of AI use in instruction. Those require additional data sources.
Why Tier 2 matters:
Description-only signals reveal AI activity that may be invisible to students, advisors, and leadership if only course titles are reviewed.
Methodology note. Scan counts were read from the “ALL Courses” tabs in the UG and GRAD spreadsheets. AI-signal records were read from four AI-signal tabs: UG “AI in Title,” UG “AI in Description Only,” GRAD “AI in Title,” and GRAD “AI in Description Only.” Tier 1 means the signal appears in the course title tab; Tier 2 means the signal appears only in the description tab. Counts are course records exactly as represented in the sheets, not enrollments and not deduplicated curriculum experiences. Distinct course titles are counted separately from course records because undergraduate/graduate pairings can share titles.
